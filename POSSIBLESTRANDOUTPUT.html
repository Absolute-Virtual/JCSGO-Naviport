Possible Careers (per Strand)

STEM
Science, Technology, Engineering, and Mathematics

Engineer - An engineer solves real-world problems by designing, building, and improving systems, structures, or technologies using math, science, and creativity.

Doctor - A doctor diagnoses and treats illnesses, promotes health, and cares for patients to improve their physical and emotional well-being.

Architect - An architect designs buildings and spaces by blending creativity, engineering, and functionality to shape environments that inspire and serve people.

Chemist - A chemist explores the properties and reactions of substances to develop new materials, medicines, and solutions that improve everyday life.

Data Analyst - A data analyst transforms raw data into meaningful insights by collecting, cleaning, and interpreting information to support smart business decisions.

Pilot - A pilot operates aircraft to transport passengers or cargo, navigating safely through airspace and weather conditions.

Biologist - A biologist studies living organisms and their environments to understand how life functions, evolves, and interacts with the world.

Statistician - A statistician collects, analyzes, and interprets data to uncover patterns, support decision-making, and solve real-world problems in fields like health, economics, and social science.

ABM
Accountancy, Business, and Management

Accountant - An accountant manages financial records, analyzes budgets, and ensures accuracy in reporting to help individuals or organizations make sound financial decisions.

Business Manager - A business manager oversees operations, leads teams, and makes strategic decisions to ensure a company runs efficiently and meets its goals.

Entrepreneur - An entrepreneur creates and builds businesses by identifying opportunities, taking risks, and turning ideas into profitable ventures.

Marketing Officer - A marketing officer promotes a company’s products or services by creating campaigns, analyzing market trends, and engaging customers to boost brand awareness and sales.

HR Specialist - An HR (Human Resources) specialist supports a company by managing employee relations, recruitment, training, and benefits to ensure a healthy and productive workplace.

Financial Analyst - A financial analyst evaluates economic data, market trends, and company performance to guide investment decisions and financial planning.

Sales Executive - A sales executive drives business growth by identifying customer needs, promoting products or services, and closing deals to boost revenue.

Economist - An economist studies how resources are produced, distributed, and consumed to understand markets and guide decision-making.

HUMSS
Humanities and Social Sciences

Guidance Counselor - A guidance counselor supports students in their academic, personal, and career growth through advice, encouragement, and planning.

Lawyer - A lawyer provides legal advice, prepares documents, and represents clients in court to uphold justice and protect rights.

Psychologist - A psychologist studies mental processes and behavior to help people understand and improve their emotional well-being.

Journalist - A journalist gathers, investigates, and reports news to inform the public through writing, video, or broadcast.

Teacher - A teacher educates, guides, and inspires students by sharing knowledge and fostering learning in the classroom.

Criminologist - A criminologist studies crime, its causes, and effects to help improve law enforcement and criminal justice systems.

Anthropologist - An anthropologist studies humans, cultures, and societies—past and present—to understand how people live and relate to one another.

Public Relations Officer - A public relations officer manages a company’s image by communicating with the public and media to build trust and maintain a positive reputation.

GAS
General Academic Strand

Teacher - A teacher educates, guides, and inspires students by sharing knowledge and fostering learning in the classroom.

Journalist - A journalist investigates, writes, and reports news stories to inform the public through print, broadcast, or digital media.

Police Officer - A police officer enforces laws, protects the public, and maintains peace and safety in the community.

Social Worker - A social worker helps people overcome challenges by providing support, resources, and advocacy for their well-being.

Lawyer - A lawyer advises clients on legal matters and represents them in court to uphold justice and protect rights.

Psychologist - A psychologist studies mental processes and behavior to help people understand and improve their emotional well-being.

Event Planner - An event planner organizes and coordinates events like weddings, parties, or conferences to ensure they run smoothly.

Public Servant - A public servant works in government to serve the community by delivering public services and upholding the law.

TVL
Technical-Vocational-Livelihood

Video Editor - A video editor cuts, arranges, and enhances video clips to create polished and engaging visual stories.

Web Developer - A web developer builds and maintains websites using coding languages to ensure functionality, design, and user experience.

Graphic Designer - A graphic designer creates visual content to communicate ideas through images, typography, and layout.

Computer Technician - A computer technician installs, maintains, and repairs computer hardware and software to keep systems running smoothly.

Cybersecurity Analyst - A cybersecurity analyst protects computer systems and networks from digital threats like hacking and malware.

Animator - An animator creates moving images or characters for films, games, or digital media using art and motion.

Digital Marketer - A digital marketer promotes products or services online using tools like social media, SEO, and ads.

Virtual Assistant - A virtual assistant is a smart helper that performs tasks and answers questions through voice or text.
